IDUKKI



IDUKKI ARCH DAM

The Idukki dam is constructed across the Periyar River, in a valley between the Kuravan and Kurathi hills in Kerala. The awe-inspiring, majestic dam, which stands at 167.68 meters (550 ft), is one of the highest arch dams in Asia. The dam, which is one of the Idukki tourist places, constructed by the Kerala government in 1975 and supports 780 MW hydroelectric power station that draws electricity from this dam.

Location: 33 km from Idukki Dist.

Timings: Public Holidays, Saturdays and Sundays

Entry fee: Rs 25 for adults and Rs 5 for children. The tariff for a 15-minute boat ride for 5 persons is Rs. 600

IDUKKI WILDLIFE SANCTUARY

Away from the hustle and bustle of the cities, the Idukki Wildlife is located in the Thodupuzha and Udumpanchola taluks of Idukki at an altitude of 450 – 750 ft above the sea level.The sanctuary, flanked on 3 sides by the tranquil waters of Idukki Lake, is housed amidst picturesque settings of tropical evergreen & semi-evergreen forests, meadows, and deciduous trees.The Wildlife Sanctuary, one of the places to visit in Idukki, houses hordes of wild elephants.

Location: 40 km from Thodupuzha, Idukki

Timings: All day

Entry fee: None.

VALARA WATERFALLS

Valara Waterfalls, is one of the places to visit in Idukki, is located on Munnar-Kochi Highway. The gushing waters from a series of waterfalls, falling from an altitude of 1000 meters provide an alluring view.Nestled in the verdant vegetation featuring various types of woods and multitude of the animal kingdom, it is an ideal place for nature lovers and bird watchers.

Location: 42 km from Munnar

Timings: This upland can be visited anytime

Entry fee: None.

MUNNAR

Munnar rises as three mountain streams merge - Mudrapuzha, Nallathanni and Kundala. 1,600 m above sea level, this hill station was once the summer resort of the erstwhile British Government in South India. Sprawling tea plantations, picturesque towns, winding lanes and holiday facilities make this a popular resort town. Among the exotic flora found in the forests and grasslands here is the Neelakurinji.

Location: 35km from Idukki

Timings: 24 hours open for all.

Entry fee: None

MEESHAPPULIMALA

The second highest peak in South India, Meesapulimala is an ideal spot for trekking. The starting point of the trek is either Rhodovalley or Silent Valley. After trekking for about 8 km from Rhodovalley, one gets to witness the grasslands, wildlife, rainforests, and small waterfalls.

Location: 39 km from city center

Timings: This place can be visited anytime, and is open for all.

Entry fee: None.

PERIYAR NATIONAL PARK

Famous for its Tiger reserves and thick vegetation, the Periyar National Park is a perfect place for nature buffs and wildlife lovers. However, the best way to explore this natural habitat is by safaris either on jeep or elephants.

Location: 32 km from city center

Timings: 6:00 AM - 7:00 PM, Night Safari after 8:00 PM

Entry fee:

Indians: Adult - INR 45, Child - INR 15

Foreigners: Adult - INR 500, Child - INR 180
